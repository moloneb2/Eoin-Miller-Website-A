Musical Background

Eoin Miller was the drummer for the group ‘My Albatross’ who were a Manchester based band whose sound was an eclectic, up-beat Indie/Rock style with complex vocals. They had their singles ‘Live and Live Lonely ', ‘Richman’s daughter’ along with the B sides of ‘I Don’t Love You’ and ‘Spending her Daisies’’ played on BBC and XFM radio during 2009 - 2010.

Current Projects

Independent solo artist Eoin Miller continues to build momentum with a growing catalogue of original music, now featuring four singles available across all major streaming platforms. Known for blending styles and experimenting with sound, Miller’s releases showcase an eclectic mix of acoustic storytelling, upbeat summer rhythms, and vintage country-rock influences.

His debut single, “Don’t Be Lonely,” introduces listeners to Miller’s poetic songwriting style. The track is an acoustic, upbeat ballad driven by warm guitar tones and lyrical rhymes that reflect themes of connection and optimism. It established Miller’s signature approach: simple instrumentation paired with thoughtful, expressive lyrics.

Following the debut, Miller released “Talk,” a bright, tap-along tune built around a playful ukulele arrangement. With its laid-back summer feel and catchy rhythm, the song captures a lighter side of Miller’s songwriting, offering an easy-going listening experience that contrasts nicely with the reflective tone of his first release.

Miller’s third single, “Unremittingly,” marks a stylistic shift. The track leans into country and rockabilly influences, bringing a richer, more roots-driven sound to his growing discography. The song received radio airplay on Dublin City FM, further expanding Miller’s reach among Irish audiences. Demonstrating his creative versatility, Miller also released the song in Irish under the title “Gan Staonadh.” With the song being played on Belfast's 107.1FM Raidió Fáilte. Together, these releases highlight Eoin Miller’s willingness to explore different musical textures while maintaining a consistent focus on storytelling and melody. With four singles now available to stream worldwide, Miller continues to develop a distinctive voice within the independent music scene.
